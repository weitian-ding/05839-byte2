Is the data correct?

If we want to study wines all over the world, the wine review dataset is biased. The sampling error comes from the fact that the dataset is scraped from WineEnthusiast. WineEnthusiast only includes wine with ratings greater than or equal to 80, which means that the sample is biased towards higher customer satisfaction wine.

We can also look at where the wine is produced (see FustionTable below). For an unbiased dataset, the sample distribution of wine origin locations should reflect the true distribution of wineries all over the world, but it would require some domain knowledge in wine to make a judgment on this.

However, if we instead treat all wine reviews on WineEnthusiast as the population, then the only source of bias comes from scrapping. We then need to examine the scrapper and answer whether scrapping errors happen randomly or they happen more frequently for certain types of review posts.

Also, there can be measurement errors. The ratings are provided by WineEnthusiast tasters. There are 15 tasters in the dataset (see table 2). FusionTable 2 shows a network graph between wine varieties and wine tasters, and we can see that there are some major contributors (blue nodes) like @vossroger, and certain varieties (orange nodes) like Pinot Noir are tasted by more tasters than others. Figure 15 shows the wine ratings given by the top 5 tasters with the largest number of reviews, we can see some difference in summary statistics. This can be due to different tasters having different expertise and they specialize in certain varieties. But there could also be noise in wine ratings introduced by pressure from wine producers or different standards between wine tasters. The wine tasting process itself can also introduce measurement errors, for example, a bad sample of wine is delivered to the taster.
